Root Cause

Why we stopped
optimizing wire length

The proxy is 1.0×wirelength + 0.5×density + 0.5×congestion. That looks balanced, but the three terms don't normalize to similar scales: congestion runs 1.3–2.7, density around 0.9, and wirelength sits at about 0.06. We tried wirelength SA anyway. Pulling macros closer together tightens routing corridors, congestion spikes and the proxy goes up, not down.

  • Wirelength SA clusters macros tightly; H/V corridors congest; proxy goes up, not down
  • initial.plc already has decent spread from the prior EDA flow; perturb it, don't replace it
  • Every move CongFlow considers is ranked by its effect on the live H/V routing congestion map
  • Density-based move ranking is anti-correlated with proxy cost, confirmed empirically on every benchmark we tested

Engine Design

Eleven phases. One objective.

Every phase uses the same evaluation loop: re-legalize the placement to resolve any overlap, then call the TILOS proxy scorer. Whatever candidate scores lowest becomes the new best. Directed phases (1–5) pick movement directions from live H/V routing congestion maps pulled straight from the evaluator. Later phases explore territory that greedy gradient descent can't reach. The final two phases (R1, R2) are relocation-based: instead of swapping macros with neighbors, they lift hot macros entirely out of their slots and place them into the lowest-congestion legal gaps available on the canvas.

0
Baseline Legalization
ALWAYS RUNS
Resolves overlaps from initial.plc using a vectorized spiral search that places macros largest-area-first. A float32 precision fix here is critical: without it, the legalizer takes a slightly different path and ibm04 diverges to 1.3364 instead of 1.3316, a difference that compounds through every phase. 12× faster than the original scalar version (3.2s → 0.27s on ibm04).
1
Iterative Congestion-Gradient Descent
PHASE 1
Up to 12 steps at frac=0.04 (each macro can move up to 4% of the canvas width per step), with the step size halved when improvement stops. After each step that lowers the proxy, the starting position and the live congestion map both update, so the next iteration works from fresh gradient information. Stops when no move helps or there's less time left than three scoring calls would take.
↓ ibm04: 1.4101 → 1.3316 via 7 iterations
2
Wide Steps from Baseline
PHASE 2
Larger steps (frac=0.08, 0.12) from baseline_pos using the stale congestion map from Phase 1's best iteration. The stale map acts as second-order curvature information, guiding larger jumps into unexplored basins.
↓ ibm02: 1.6800 → 1.5823
3
Best-Position Cong-Grad
PHASE 3
Perturbs the current best_pl at frac=0.04 using a cached congestion map. Finds basins missed by Phases 1/2. ibm04's stable 1.3316 win lives specifically in this basin; it requires starting from the Phase 1 best rather than the original baseline.
5
Async DREAMPlace: Best-of-Both
PHASE 5 · v2 ENHANCED
Two DREAMPlace instances launch at place() entry and run concurrently with Phases 1–3. "fixed" pins soft macros at their initial positions; "movable" lets DREAMPlace's NLP optimizer reposition the standard-cell clusters. On designs where canvas utilization is below 85%, moving the stdcell clusters into open space directly relieves congestion hotspots, which is the source of the −0.121 on ibm06. Best-of-both selection discards whichever result scores worse, so we don't regress on denser benchmarks where the movable config overshoots.
↓ −0.0161 avg · ibm06: −0.121, ibm03: −0.103, ibm02: −0.051
7
DP-Rescue Cong-Grad Chain
PHASE 7
Once the time-filling noise loop finishes, each completed DREAMPlace result gets fed into a short cong-grad chain (up to 3 iterations). DREAMPlace can land macros in positions far from initial.plc: configurations Phase 1's greedy descent can't reach on its own, which opens up congestion basins that wouldn't otherwise be explored. Win rate across the suite: 19%. Notable: ibm10 −0.045, ibm02 −0.035, ibm04 −0.018.
8
TOP-K Cong-Grad + Congestion-Aware 2-opt
PHASE 8 · S9 · NEW IN v2
Two things run here. First, TOP-K cong-grad: instead of perturbing every macro, find the K that contribute most to current congestion and move only those. Second, proxy-scored 2-opt with congestion-aware ordering (S9): macros are considered for swapping in hot-first order so the most damaging placements get the first shot at improvement. When no local neighbor swap helps, a cold-teleport swap is attempted with a macro in the lowest-congestion region to break symmetry. v1 used displacement distance to rank swaps, which turns out to be anti-correlated with proxy cost and burned the full budget making placements worse.
↓ A1+S9: −0.0061 avg · All 17 benchmarks improve
9
Random-Order Legalization
PHASE 9 · NEW IN v2
Shuffles the macro placement order in the spiral-search legalizer, exploring alternative macro assignment topologies. The greedy spiral-search is order-sensitive; different orderings produce different legal arrangements from the same initial positions.
↓ −0.0003 avg
R1
Congestion-Directed Relocation
R1 · NEW IN v2
2-opt has a structural blind spot: it can only swap macro A with a neighbor B. If no neighbor B exists that reduces congestion when swapped with the hottest macro, 2-opt is stuck in a local minimum it cannot escape by swapping alone.

Relocation breaks this constraint. For each macro ranked by congestion contribution, the pass lifts it entirely out of its current slot and scans every empty legal gap on the canvas for a lower-congestion landing spot. Every candidate position is evaluated with the incremental scorer before committing. If no gap beats the current score, the macro stays put. Because relocation is not constrained to neighbors, it can reposition a hot macro across the full canvas, opening routing corridors that swap-based moves cannot reach.
↓ −0.0096 avg · All 17 benchmarks improve
R2
Interleaved Relocation + 2-opt
R2 · v2 FINAL
Running one pass of R1 relocation after one pass of 2-opt is not the optimum order. Each phase creates new opportunities for the other:

Relocation opens 2-opt targets. Moving a hot macro to a new location changes the local neighborhood landscape. Neighbor pairs that were suboptimal swaps before the relocation can become productive ones after it.

2-opt opens relocation targets. Swapping two macros changes which cells are empty and which are congested. A macro that had no better landing spot before a 2-opt round may have a clear low-congestion gap after it.

R2 exploits this by alternating the two passes in a loop of up to 6 rounds, budget-gated. Both phases accept only on a confirmed proxy improvement from the real incremental evaluator, so the loop is strictly non-regressing. It stops as soon as neither phase finds any improvement in a full round.
↓ −0.0083 avg · ibm04 −0.043 · ibm10 −0.022 · ibm02 −0.015 · All 17 improve

Engineering Decisions

Six changes. −0.0586 avg improvement.

v2 started from a 1.4804 checkpoint. Each decision below was motivated by a concrete measured failure in the prior version, not by intuition. R2 is the final shipped change.

🎯
2-opt scored by the real proxy
Issue A1 · _two_opt_proxy_swap

v1's 2-opt accepted swaps when per-pair displacement decreased. Displacement has no relation to proxy cost. On ibm01/04/10 it actively raised proxy while appearing to "improve" things. v2 tentatively applies each swap, scores it with the real evaluator, and accepts only if cost strictly decreases.

↓ −0.0059 avg · All 17 benchmarks improve
🔀
Best-of-Both DREAMPlace
Issue A2 · soft_macros_movable=True

Two DREAMPlace configs run in parallel: "fixed" pins soft macros at their initial positions; "movable" lets DP's NLP optimizer reposition them. On benchmarks with canvas utilization below 85%, the movable config spreads standard-cell clusters away from congested corridors, which is the source of the −0.121 on ibm06. Best-of-both discards whichever result scores worse, so dense benchmarks like ibm01 and ibm09 don't regress.

↓ −0.0161 avg · ibm06 −0.121, ibm03 −0.103, ibm02 −0.051
⚡
4-Phase Incremental Scorer
Issue B3 (phases 1–4) · IncrementalScorer

Four successive speedups: (1) global position cache eliminates Python get_pos loops; (2) per-net HPWL with macro→nets index updates only touched nets; (3) numpy ABU skips .tolist() overhead; (4) per-net incremental routing footprint cache. More 2-opt iterations per budget window directly translates to better scores.

22ms → 5ms per score · --all wall-clock −155s
🌡️
TOP-K Congestion-Gradient Phase
Issue A6 · Phase 8 TOP-K cong-grad

After Phase 7, identifies the K macros with the highest per-cell congestion contribution and applies a targeted cong-grad perturbation from best_pl. Focuses the perturbation budget on the macros actually causing the congestion bottleneck rather than scattering gradient across the full canvas.

↓ −0.0010 avg · Wins on ibm02/03/04/06/16
⏱️
Cumulative Wall-Clock Guard
Issue B1 · cumulative_elapsed tracking

v1's per-benchmark budget didn't account for cumulative --all runtime, causing ibm17 to consistently exceed the 3600s harness cap and return a suboptimal early-exit placement. v2 tracks total elapsed time across all benchmarks and tightens remaining per-benchmark budgets accordingly.

ibm17 timeout eliminated · --all completes in ~480s
📍
Congestion-Directed Relocation
R1 · _relocation_moves

After 2-opt, the hottest macros can be stuck because no valid swap partner exists nearby. Relocation breaks that constraint: it lifts each hot macro entirely out of its current slot and places it into the emptiest legal gap on the canvas. Every candidate is scored with the real incremental evaluator before committing; if no gap beats the current score, the macro stays where it is.

↓ −0.0096 avg · All 17 benchmarks improve
🔁
Interleaved Relocation + 2-opt
R2 · interleaved rounds · v2 FINAL

Running relocation once after 2-opt leaves gains on the table: each relocation creates new open gaps that 2-opt can exploit, and each 2-opt pass creates new congestion patterns that relocation can target. R2 alternates the two in a loop of up to 6 rounds. Budget-gated and strictly non-regressing — both accept only on a confirmed true-proxy improvement.

↓ −0.0083 avg · ibm04 −0.043 · ibm10 −0.022 · All 17 improve
